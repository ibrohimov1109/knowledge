GOLDEN-VALUED ADVICE

Principles of strong leadership

The advice below includes principles that are essential for strong leadership, healthy thinking, and building a sustainable business. They are experience-based and, when applied consistently in life and work, lead to significant results.

1. Strive to become a strong leader

Your team always draws strength from you. The stronger you are, the stronger your team becomes.

2. Manage emotions and feelings

Emotions and feelings drive growth. If either is lacking, build a solid knowledge base about your business and products. Emotion turns into action through trust and decision-making.

3. Increase the number of loyal customers

You will eventually lose customers who constantly compare you with others. Treat them lightly, but do not choose them as long-term partners.

4. People fall into two categories

The first immediately act on ideas and tasks they hear or see. The second only listen and say “yes, I know,” but never take action.

5. Those who see far ahead become winners

6. Unite your team

Let them listen to each other and compensate for one another’s weaknesses.

7. Teach how to fish instead of giving fish

8. “Keep your glass empty”

When the glass is full, the mind becomes blocked and cannot absorb new knowledge. Advice: learn one thing, then teach one thing.

9. Do not overinvest effort in someone who is not ready

10. Systematize your business

This way, you will be able to manage everything.

11. Who is a strong leader?

A leader who, when shown one corner, can find the other three on their own.

12. When you are strong, you can recognize the strong

13. Make a choice without alternatives

14. Do not tie your business to any single person

15. Learn to agree and accept

If necessary, learn to let go of what you knew yesterday. This will help you grow many times over.

16. Be able to make decisions

Be firm, respect your decision, and stay loyal to it.

17. Never give up

Even when you cannot see the outcome, God is always with you. Those who go to the end are rewarded.

18. You find what you seek

What are you looking for?

19. Identify your 2–3 core strengths

And focus on developing them.

20. Never compare your leaders

21. Do not be afraid to make mistakes

A leader grows through mistakes.

22. Consult regularly with your mentees

23. If you want to build a strong business, forget the past and excuses

Start working on thoughts like “this is not for me” or “I am afraid.” The key to success lies exactly here.

24. Put yourself in order before organizing the group

Your mentees follow what you do, not what you say.

25. When a problem arises, try to solve it

If it cannot be solved, learn to let it go.

26. Work entrusted to God is always beneficial

Never hesitate.

27. Clearly state your goal when attending training

Repeat it internally at least 5–10 times. Otherwise, the training will not benefit you.

28. Inspiration and ideas come during work

Capture them, expand on them, and work on them — results will follow.

29. Learn to work according to level

Assign tasks to your team members based on their conditions, character, and abilities.

30. A person can only see what the mind allows

Our actions and understanding do not go beyond our mindset. Remove the “know-it-all” hat.

31. There is no laziness

It only means you have not found a goal that inspires you. Define your goal clearly.
